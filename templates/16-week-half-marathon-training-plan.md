# 16-Week Half Marathon Training Plan (Intermediate Level)

## Plan Overview

**Target Audience:** Runners with 1-2 years consistent running, 30-35 mpw base
**Goal:** Half Marathon (13.1 miles) in 16 weeks
**Weekly Mileage:** 35-50 miles
**Experience Required:** Comfortable running 60+ minutes continuously, completed at least one 10K race

## Before You Start

1. **Determine Your Current Fitness:**
   - Run a 10K time trial OR
   - Use recent 5K or 10K race time
   - Enter into McMillan or VDOT calculator

2. **Know Your Training Paces:**
   - **Easy:** Conversational pace (Zone 2, 60-70% max HR)
   - **Marathon Pace (MP):** Steady effort (Zone 3, 70-80% max HR)
   - **Tempo:** Comfortably hard (~10K-HM race pace, Zone 4, 80-90% max HR)
   - **Interval:** Hard effort (~5K race pace, Zone 5, 90-100% max HR)
   - **Half Marathon Pace (HMP):** Goal race pace

3. **Schedule Your Race:**
   - Pick your goal half marathon for Week 16
   - Optional: tune-up 10K race in Week 12

## Training Philosophy

The half marathon is the perfect balance of speed and endurance. This plan emphasizes:

- **Weeks 1-6:** Base building (volume and aerobic development)
- **Weeks 7-12:** Build phase (tempo work and threshold development)
- **Weeks 13-15:** Peak phase (race-specific training)
- **Week 16:** Taper and race

**Key focus:** Lactate threshold development + long run endurance + race pace practice

## Workout Descriptions

### Easy Runs
- **Pace:** Conversational, full sentences
- **HR:** Zone 2 (60-70% max)
- **Purpose:** Build aerobic base, recovery
- **Note:** Should feel genuinely easy

### Strides
- **Distance:** 100 meters
- **Pace:** Smooth acceleration to mile pace
- **Recovery:** Walk back
- **Purpose:** Form, neuromuscular coordination
- **Frequency:** 2-3x per week

### Marathon Pace (MP) Runs
- **Pace:** Steady effort, 30-45 sec/mile slower than tempo
- **HR:** Zone 3 (70-80% max)
- **Purpose:** Build aerobic endurance at faster pace
- **Note:** Important for half marathon - it's slightly easier than race pace

### Tempo Runs (Threshold Pace)
- **Pace:** "Comfortably hard" - roughly 10K to HM race pace
- **HR:** Zone 4 (80-90% max)
- **Feel:** Can speak 3-5 words
- **Purpose:** Improve lactate threshold (CRITICAL FOR HALF MARATHON)
- **Note:** Most important workout type for this distance

### Half Marathon Pace (HMP) Runs
- **Pace:** Goal half marathon race pace
- **Purpose:** Train body/mind for race effort, practice fueling
- **Note:** Practice race-day nutrition and pacing

### Intervals (VO2 Max Pace)
- **Pace:** 5K race pace
- **HR:** Zone 5 (90-100% max)
- **Purpose:** Maintain speed and VO2 max
- **Note:** Secondary to tempo work for half marathon

### Long Runs
- **Types:**
  - Easy long runs: All easy pace
  - MP long runs: Sections at marathon pace
  - HMP long runs: Final miles at half marathon pace
- **Purpose:** Build endurance, mental toughness, race specificity
- **Peak:** 18 miles (Week 14)

### Recovery Runs
- **Pace:** Very easy, slower than easy pace
- **Purpose:** Active recovery
- **Note:** Especially after long runs and tempo workouts

## Half Marathon Racing Strategy

### Race Week:
- **7 days out:** Last hard workout (Wednesday)
- **5-6 days out:** Easy running only
- **2-3 days out:** Short easy run with strides
- **1-2 days out:** Rest or very short shakeout

### Race Day Warm-Up:
- 10-15 min easy jog
- 6-8x100m strides
- Dynamic stretches
- Bathroom break
- Line up 5-10 min before start

### Race Pacing Strategy:

**Conservative Strategy (Recommended for First Half):**
- **Miles 1-3:** 5-10 sec/mile SLOWER than goal pace (resist the crowd!)
- **Miles 4-10:** Settle into goal pace, find rhythm
- **Miles 11-12:** Push the pace if feeling good
- **Mile 13 + 0.1:** Empty the tank, strong finish

**Even Pace Strategy (Experienced Runners):**
- Target even splits throughout
- Slight negative split if possible
- Save energy for final 5K push

**Common Pacing Mistakes:**
- Going out too fast (most common!)
- Not accounting for hills in early miles
- Ignoring effort level on warm/windy days

### Fueling Strategy:
- **Pre-race:** Light meal 2-3 hours before (test in training)
- **During race:**
  - Most runners: Water/sports drink at aid stations
  - 1:30+ finish time: Consider 1-2 gels (Mile 6 and 10)
  - Practice in long runs!
- **Post-race:** Carbs + protein within 30-60 min

### Post-Race Recovery:
- Cool-down: 10-15 min easy walk/jog
- Easy running only for 5-7 days
- No hard workouts for 10-14 days
- Rule of thumb: 1 day easy for every mile raced (13 days)

## Customization Guidelines

### If You're New to This Distance:
- Start with 30-32 mpw base instead of 35
- Reduce long runs by 1-2 miles
- Focus on completing plan, not hitting exact paces
- Don't add extra miles or workouts

### If You're More Advanced:
- Can start at Week 3-4 if coming off solid base
- Can add 3-5 miles to weekly total
- Can extend long runs by 1-2 miles (max 20 miles)
- Don't exceed 50 mpw for this plan

### If You Miss a Workout:
1. **Easy run:** Skip it
2. **Long run:** Reschedule within same week if possible
3. **Tempo run:** Most important - try to reschedule
4. **Intervals:** Can skip, less critical for half marathon
5. **Never:** Double up quality days

### If You're Too Tired:
- Make hard workout an easy run
- Take extra rest day
- Check if easy runs are truly easy
- Recovery > single workout

## Training Intensity Distribution

**This plan follows 80/20:**
- ~75% easy running (Zones 1-2)
- ~15% tempo/threshold (Zone 4)
- ~5% marathon pace (Zone 3)
- ~5% intervals (Zone 5)

The half marathon is primarily a **lactate threshold race**, so tempo work is heavily emphasized.

## Success Tips

1. **Long runs are crucial** - Don't skip these
2. **Tempo runs build race fitness** - Your most important workout
3. **Practice race pace** - Get comfortable with HMP in Weeks 13-15
4. **Nail your fueling** - Test nutrition strategy in training
5. **Don't go out too fast** - Hardest lesson to learn
6. **Respect the taper** - Trust your training in final 2 weeks
7. **Stay consistent** - Better to do 90% healthy than 110% injured
8. **Sleep matters** - 7-9 hours nightly

## Mental Training

The half marathon is as much mental as physical:
- **Miles 1-7:** Stay patient, resist going too fast
- **Miles 7-10:** This is where the race really starts, stay focused
- **Miles 10-13:** Mental toughness matters, embrace the discomfort
- **Practice mental strategies in long runs**
- **Break race into 5K segments**

## Expected Adaptations

By end of this plan:
- Significantly improved lactate threshold
- Enhanced endurance for 90+ minute efforts
- Better running economy
- Improved fat oxidation
- Mental toughness for distance racing
- Race-specific fitness for 13.1 miles
