# 18-Week Marathon Training Plan (Intermediate Level)

## Plan Overview

**Target Audience:** Runners with 2+ years consistent running, 35-40 mpw base, completed at least one half marathon
**Goal:** Marathon (26.2 miles) in 18 weeks
**Weekly Mileage:** 16-68 miles (peak week 68)
**Experience Required:** Comfortable running 90+ minutes continuously, solid aerobic base

### If This Is More Volume Than You Want

This plan peaks at **68 miles** in its biggest week. That is a real marathon build, and
it sits at the top of the intermediate range rather than the middle of it. Every weekly
total in this plan has been checked against the workouts actually listed underneath it,
so the number above is the number you will run.

**To run this plan at roughly 55 miles per week instead:**

- Trim **6-8 miles per week off the easy days only**. Take a mile off two or three easy
  runs rather than a large chunk off one.
- **Do not cut the quality sessions** (tempo, intervals, marathon-pace work). They are the
  reason the plan works.
- **Do not cut the long run** by more than a mile or two. It is the other reason.
- If the plan still feels like too much after that, you want the beginner version, not a
  trimmed version of this one. There is no shame in that and it will make you faster than
  limping through a plan that is over your head.

The volume is a dial. The easy/hard structure is not. See
[Training Principles](../fundamentals/training-principles.md) and
[Injury Prevention](../fundamentals/injury-prevention.md).

## Before You Start

1. **Determine Your Current Fitness:**
   - Use recent half marathon time (preferred) OR
   - Use 10K time for estimate
   - Enter into McMillan or VDOT calculator

2. **Know Your Training Paces:**
   - **Easy:** Conversational pace (Zone 2, 60-70% max HR)
   - **Marathon Pace (MP):** Goal race pace (Zone 3, 70-80% max HR)
   - **Tempo:** Comfortably hard (~10K pace, Zone 4, 80-90% max HR)
   - **Interval:** Hard effort (~5K pace, Zone 5, 90-100% max HR)

3. **Schedule Your Race:**
   - Pick your goal marathon for Week 18
   - Optional: tune-up half marathon in Week 14

4. **Medical Clearance:**
   - Marathon training is demanding
   - Consult physician if you have any health concerns
   - Build gradually, listen to your body

## Training Philosophy

The marathon is won or lost in training. Success requires:
- Strong aerobic base
- Long run progression
- Marathon pace familiarity
- Smart recovery

**Training Phases:**
- **Weeks 1-8:** Base building (focus on volume and aerobic development)
- **Weeks 9-14:** Build phase (add quality, marathon pace work)
- **Weeks 15-17:** Peak phase (race-specific training)
- **Week 18:** Taper and race

**Key Principle:** The long run is king for marathon training. Everything else supports it.

## Workout Descriptions

### Easy Runs
- **Pace:** Conversational, full sentences
- **HR:** Zone 2 (60-70% max)
- **Purpose:** Build aerobic base, recovery
- **Critical:** For marathon, easy must be truly easy (often 60-90 sec/mile slower than MP)

### Strides
- **Distance:** 100 meters
- **Pace:** Smooth to mile race pace
- **Purpose:** Maintain leg speed, neuromuscular efficiency
- **Frequency:** 2-3x per week

### Marathon Pace (MP) Runs
- **Pace:** Goal marathon race pace
- **HR:** Zone 3 (70-80% max)
- **Purpose:** Train body/mind for race effort, practice fueling
- **Critical:** Most important workout for marathon - practice pacing and nutrition

### Tempo Runs (Threshold Pace)
- **Pace:** "Comfortably hard" - roughly 10K to HM pace
- **HR:** Zone 4 (80-90% max)
- **Purpose:** Improve lactate threshold, mental toughness
- **Note:** Important but secondary to MP work for marathon

### Intervals (VO2 Max Pace)
- **Pace:** 5K race pace
- **Purpose:** Maintain speed, improve VO2 max
- **Note:** Used sparingly in marathon training - maintains speed without excess stress

### Long Runs
- **Types:**
  - Easy long runs: All easy pace (early weeks)
  - MP long runs: Finish with marathon pace miles (build weeks)
  - Goal: Build endurance, practice race pace, test fueling
- **Peak:** 22 miles (Week 15)

### Recovery Runs
- **Pace:** Very easy, slower than easy runs
- **Purpose:** Active recovery, especially after long runs
- **Critical:** Day after 18+ mile run should be easy recovery

## Marathon Racing Strategy

### The Final Two Weeks:
- **Week 17:** Cut volume by ~25%, maintain some quality
- **Week 18:** Cut volume by ~50%, short easy runs + strides
- **Last hard workout:** 10 days before race
- **Last long run:** 14 days before race (12 miles)

### Race Week Details:
- **Sunday (8 days out):** Last long run (12 miles easy)
- **Monday-Thursday:** Short easy runs, strides
- **Friday:** Rest or 20 min very easy
- **Saturday:** Rest or 15-20 min shakeout with strides
- **Sunday:** RACE

### Pre-Race Preparation:
- **3 days before:** Start carb-loading (increase carbs, reduce fiber)
- **2 days before:** Continue carbs, stay hydrated, reduce fiber
- **1 day before:** Light carbs, hydrate, avoid new foods, early dinner
- **Morning of:** Light breakfast 2-3 hours before (tested in training!)

### Race Day Warm-Up:
- 10-15 min easy jog (don't overdo it)
- 4-6x100m strides
- Dynamic stretches
- Bathroom
- Line up 10-15 min early

### Marathon Pacing Strategy:

**Conservative Strategy (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED for first marathon):**
- **Miles 1-6:** 10-15 sec/mile SLOWER than goal pace (hardest discipline!)
- **Miles 7-13:** Ease into goal pace, assess how you feel
- **Miles 14-18:** Hold goal pace, stay mentally strong
- **Miles 19-23:** This is the race - mental toughness critical
- **Miles 24-26.2:** Give what you have left

**The Wall:**
- Typically hits Miles 18-22
- Caused by glycogen depletion
- Avoid by: proper training, pacing, fueling
- If you hit it: slow down, walk if needed, finish strong

**Even Pace Strategy (Experienced Marathoners):**
- Aim for even splits
- Slight negative split if conditions allow
- Adjust for hills, wind, heat

### Fueling Strategy:

**Before Race:**
- Breakfast 2-3 hours before
- Tested meal (never try new foods!)
- Example: Bagel with peanut butter + banana + water

**During Race:**
- **Water:** Every aid station (small sips early, more later)
- **Sports drink:** Alternate with water, or every other station
- **Gels/Fuel:**
  - Start at Mile 6-8
  - Every 45-60 minutes (roughly every 4-5 miles)
  - Practice this in long runs!
  - Take with water, not sports drink
- **Total fuel:** 4-6 gels for most runners

**Common Fueling Mistakes:**
- Waiting until Mile 15-18 (too late!)
- Not practicing in training
- Trying new products on race day
- Not taking in enough calories

### Post-Race Recovery:

**Immediate:**
- Walk for 10-15 minutes
- Stretch gently
- Eat/drink within 30 min (carbs + protein)
- Keep moving periodically

**Week 1:**
- Complete rest or very easy walking/swimming
- No running for 3-5 days minimum
- Focus on sleep, nutrition, hydration

**Week 2:**
- Easy running only, 20-30 min
- No more than every other day
- Listen to your body

**Weeks 3-4:**
- Gradual return to easy running
- No hard workouts for 3-4 weeks minimum
- Rule: 1 day recovery for every mile raced (26 days easy!)

## Customization Guidelines

### If This Is Your First Marathon:
- Consider adding 2-4 weeks to base phase
- Be conservative with MP segments in long runs
- Focus on finishing, not time goal
- Start 10-15 sec/mile slower than predicted pace

### If You're More Experienced:
- Can peak at 60-65 miles if your base supports it
- Can extend peak long run to 23 miles
- More aggressive MP practice
- Can add a midweek medium-long run (10-12 miles)

### If You Miss a Workout:
1. **Easy run:** Skip it
2. **Long run:** Absolutely reschedule if possible - MOST IMPORTANT
3. **MP workout:** Important - try to reschedule
4. **Tempo/Intervals:** Can skip if needed
5. **Never:** Stack long run and quality in same 48 hours

## Training Intensity Distribution

**Marathon plan follows 80/20:**
- ~80% easy running (Zones 1-2)
- ~10-12% marathon pace (Zone 3)
- ~5-8% tempo (Zone 4)
- ~2-3% intervals (Zone 5)

**The marathon is primarily an aerobic race - easy miles are crucial!**

## Success Tips

1. **Respect the long run** - Never skip, always prioritize
2. **Easy runs truly easy** - Most important for marathon training
3. **Practice marathon pace** - Must be comfortable with race pace
4. **Nail your fueling** - Test everything in training
5. **Never go out too fast** - Discipline in first 10K pays off
6. **Respect the taper** - Trust your training, rest is productive
7. **Stay healthy** - Consistent 95% plan beats injured 100% plan
8. **Sleep 8-9 hours** - Recovery needs increase with marathon training

## Mental Preparation

The marathon is 50% mental:
- **Visualization:** Picture yourself executing race plan
- **Break it down:** Think in 10K or 5-mile segments
- **Positive self-talk:** Prepare mantras for tough miles
- **Expect discomfort:** Miles 18-23 will hurt - plan for it
- **Trust your training:** You've put in the work

## Common Marathon Training Mistakes

1. **Running easy days too fast** → Chronic fatigue, poor long runs
2. **Going out too fast on race day** → The wall at Mile 20
3. **Skipping MP practice** → Race pace feels foreign
4. **Inadequate fueling practice** → GI issues or bonking
5. **Not respecting recovery** → Injury, overtraining
6. **Doing too much in taper** → Arriving fatigued
7. **Trying new things on race day** → Blisters, GI distress
