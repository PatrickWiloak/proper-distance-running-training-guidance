# 12-Week 5K Training Plan (Intermediate Level)

## Plan Overview

**Target Audience:** Runners with 6+ months consistent running, 20-25 mpw base
**Goal:** 5K race in 12 weeks
**Weekly Mileage:** 25-35 miles
**Experience Required:** Comfortable running 30-40 minutes continuously

## Before You Start

1. **Determine Your Current Fitness:**
   - Run a 5K time trial OR
   - Use recent race time (any distance)
   - Enter into McMillan or VDOT calculator

2. **Know Your Training Paces:**
   - **Easy:** Conversational pace (Zone 2, 60-70% max HR)
   - **Tempo:** Comfortably hard (~10K race pace, Zone 4)
   - **Interval:** Hard effort (~5K race pace, Zone 5)
   - **Recovery:** Very easy (Zone 1-2)

3. **Schedule Your Race:**
   - Pick your goal 5K race for Week 12
   - Optional: tune-up race in Week 8

## Training Philosophy

- **Weeks 1-4:** Base building (mostly easy miles)
- **Weeks 5-8:** Build phase (add tempo work)
- **Weeks 9-11:** Peak phase (tempo + intervals)
- **Week 12:** Taper and race

## Workout Descriptions

### Easy Runs
- **Pace:** Conversational, can speak in full sentences
- **HR:** Zone 2 (60-70% max)
- **Purpose:** Build aerobic base, recover from hard days
- **Note:** These should feel genuinely easy

### Strides
- **Distance:** 100 meters
- **Pace:** Smooth acceleration to ~mile race pace, not all-out
- **Recovery:** Walk back to start
- **Purpose:** Improve form, neuromuscular coordination
- **Note:** Do after easy runs, 2-3x per week

### Tempo Runs
- **Pace:** "Comfortably hard" - roughly 10K race pace
- **HR:** Zone 4 (80-90% max)
- **Feel:** Can speak 3-5 word phrases
- **Purpose:** Improve lactate threshold
- **Note:** Should be sustainable for the duration

### Intervals
- **Pace:** 5K race pace or slightly faster
- **HR:** Zone 5 (90-100% max)
- **Recovery:** Light jog, should feel ~80% recovered before next interval
- **Purpose:** Improve VO2 max
- **Note:** If you can't complete workout, you're going too fast

### Long Runs
- **Pace:** Easy pace, sometimes with tempo finish
- **Purpose:** Build endurance, mental toughness
- **Note:** Can add tempo/MP sections in later weeks

### Recovery Runs
- **Pace:** Very easy, slower than normal easy pace
- **Purpose:** Active recovery, promote blood flow
- **Note:** Should feel almost too easy

## Customization Guidelines

### If You Need to Miss a Workout:
1. **Miss easy run:** No big deal, skip it
2. **Miss long run:** Shift schedule, don't skip
3. **Miss quality (tempo/intervals):** Do it next day if possible, or skip and continue
4. **Never:** Make up missed workouts by doubling up quality days

### If You're Too Tired:
- Make any hard workout an easy run
- Recovery is more important than one workout
- Take an extra rest day if needed

### If Workouts Feel Too Easy:
- Don't increase volume and intensity simultaneously
- Add 1-2 miles to easy runs first
- Only increase quality if recovering well

### If Workouts Feel Too Hard:
- Slow down easy runs (most common issue)
- Reduce tempo/interval pace slightly
- Add extra recovery day
- Check if you calculated paces correctly

## Success Tips

1. **Easy days truly easy** - Most important rule
2. **Hard days hard enough** - Don't hold back on quality
3. **Sleep 7-9 hours** - Recovery happens during sleep
4. **Stay consistent** - Better to do 80% of plan than 100% then get injured
5. **Listen to your body** - Better to take extra rest than push into injury
6. **Track your training** - Log miles, paces, how you felt
7. **Nutrition matters** - Fuel your training properly
8. **Trust the taper** - Don't panic when you reduce volume in Week 12

## Race Week Strategy

### Days Before Race:
- **5-7 days out:** Last hard workout (Wednesday of race week)
- **3-4 days out:** Easy running only
- **2 days out:** Short easy run with a few strides
- **1 day out:** Rest or very short easy jog

### Race Day:
- **Warm-up:** 10-15 min easy, 4-6x100m strides, drills
- **Race strategy:** First mile controlled, settle into rhythm, last mile push
- **Cool-down:** 10-15 min easy jog

### After Race:
- Easy running for 1-2 weeks
- No hard workouts for at least 1 week
- Reflect on what worked, what to improve
